Our Top 25 Recommended games From different Genre



  1. Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO)



    Market Size and Player Base: Counter-Strike: Global Offensive is an FPS Game, developed by Valve Corporation, has sustained a massive player base since its release in 2012. With its tactical gameplay and competitive modes, CS:GO boasts over 25 million monthly active players. Revenue and Profit: CS:GO’s free-to-play model, coupled with its in-game marketplace for skins and cosmetics, has led to significant revenue generation. In 2020, CS:GO’s estimated revenue exceeded $1 billion, marking its robust profitability. Analysis: CS:GO’s longevity can be attributed to its balanced gameplay, competitive matchmaking, and the enduring appeal of its esports scene. Regular updates, including new maps and game modes, have maintained player engagement. The game’s integration with the Steam platform and its dedicated esports ecosystem have bolstered its revenue and popularity.


  2. Call of Duty: Modern Warfare



    Market Size and Player Base: The Call of Duty franchise has consistently attracted millions of players. The 2019 release FPS Game, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare, revitalized the series with its gripping narrative and polished gameplay, amassing millions of players within weeks. Revenue and Profit: Call of Duty: Modern Warfare adopted a battle pass system, offering in-game cosmetics and additional content. Its revenue in 2019 surpassed $600 million within the first three days of release, making it one of the highest-grossing Call of Duty titles. Analysis: Modern Warfare’s success can be attributed to its brand recognition, high-quality graphics, and engaging single-player campaign. The introduction of cross-platform play expanded its player base, fostering a more vibrant multiplayer ecosystem. By embracing live-service models, Modern Warfare successfully monetized post-launch content.


  3. Overwatch



    Market Size and Player Base: Overwatch is an FPS Game, developed by Blizzard Entertainment, stormed onto the scene in 2016, attracting millions of players with its vibrant characters and team-based gameplay. While exact player counts are not consistently disclosed, Overwatch’s community remains strong. Revenue and Profit: Overwatch employs a loot box system for cosmetic items, contributing to its revenue stream. The game’s revenue exceeded $1 billion by 2019, bolstered by its growing esports scene and merchandise sales. Analysis: Overwatch’s success stems from its diverse cast of heroes, each offering unique playstyles and abilities. Blizzard’s focus on esports with the Overwatch League has cultivated a competitive community. However, criticism regarding loot box mechanics has sparked debates about the ethics of microtransactions in gaming.


  4. Destiny 2



    Market Size and Player Base: Destiny 2 is an FPS Game, developed by Bungie, combines FPS gameplay with MMO elements, attracting a dedicated player base. Its transition to a free-to-play model in 2019 expanded its reach. Revenue and Profit: Destiny 2 offers expansions and in-game purchases through its Eververse store. The game’s revenue reached $300 million in its first month after transitioning to free-to-play. Analysis: Destiny 2’s fusion of FPS mechanics and RPG elements has resonated with players seeking a dynamic online experience. Its evolving narrative and regular content updates have maintained player engagement. However, the game’s monetization strategies, particularly the Eververse store, have received criticism from players.


  5. Apex Legends



    Market Size and Player Base: Apex Legends is an FPS Game, developed by Respawn Entertainment, burst onto the battle royale scene in 2019, garnering 50 million players within its first month. Revenue and Profit: Apex Legends monetizes through a battle pass and cosmetic items. In its first year, the game generated over $600 million in revenue. Analysis: Apex Legends’ success lies in its fast-paced gameplay, unique characters with distinct abilities, and team-oriented mechanics. The surprise launch, coupled with Respawn’s reputation, contributed to its rapid growth. While it faces stiff competition in the battle royale genre, regular updates and events have sustained its player base.


  6. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt



    This open-world RPG is known for its rich narrative, stunning graphics, and deep character development. It's set in a dark fantasy world and follows the adventures of Geralt of Rivia.


  7. The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim - Skyrim



    an open-world RPG known for its vast and immersive world, with numerous quests, dungeons, and a rich lore. Players can customize their character and explore the land of Tamriel.


  8. Genshin Impact



    an open-world, action role-playing game that allows the player to control one of four interchangeable characters in a party. Switching between characters can be done quickly during combat, allowing the player to use several different combinations of skills and attacks.


  9. Final Fantasy



    a console and computer RPG game series created by Hironobu Sakaguchi and produced by Square Enix (formerly Square Co., Ltd.). Final Fantasy is the most widely distributed game series of all time, including standard RPG games for consoles, portable games, MMORPGs, games for mobile phones, three animated feature films and two feature-length CGI films.


  10. Assassin Creed



    an action-adventure and stealth video game series created by Patrice Désilets, Jade Raymond and Corey May, developed and published by Ubisoft using the Anvil game engine. The game series depicts the fight between the Assassins, who fight for peace by free will, and the Templars, who want peace through being under power. The game series features historical fiction, science fiction, and fictional characters, blended with historical events and figures. Players will control Assassin characters in the past most of the time, while they can also play as Desmond Miles or an Assassin Initiate in the present, hunting down Templar targets.


  11. FIFA 23



    a football video game published by EA Sports. It is the 30th installment in the FIFA series that is developed by EA Sports, the final installment under the FIFA banner, and released worldwide on 30 September 2022 for Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Windows, Xbox One and Xbox Series X/S Kylian Mbappé is the cover athlete for the standard and legacy editions. Mbappé shares the cover of the ultimate edition with Sam Kerr. Listed in Guinness World Records as the best-selling sports video game franchise in the world, the game is the final under the 29-year partnership between EA and FIFA. Future football games by EA are named under the banner of EA Sports FC (alternatively just "EAFC").


  12. NBA 2K23



    a 2022 basketball video game developed by Visual Concepts and published by 2K, based on the National Basketball Association (NBA). It is the 24th installment in the NBA 2K franchise, the successor to NBA 2K22 and the predecessor to NBA 2K24. The game was released on September 9, 2022 for Microsoft Windows, Nintendo Switch, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One and Xbox Series X/S. The NBA 2K23 Arcade Edition was released on Apple Arcade on October 18, 2022.


  13. Rocket League



    a vehicular soccer video game developed and published by Psyonix. The game was first released for PlayStation 4 and Windows in July 2015, with ports for Xbox One and Nintendo Switch being released later on. In June 2016, 505 Games began distributing a physical retail version for PlayStation 4 and Xbox One, with Warner Bros. Interactive Entertainment taking over those duties by the end of 2017. Versions for macOS and Linux were also released in 2016, but support for their online services were dropped in 2020. The game went free-to-play in September 2020.


  14. Gran Turismo (GT)



    a series of racing simulation video games developed by Polyphony Digital. Released on PlayStation systems, Gran Turismo games are intended to emulate the appearance and performance of a large selection of vehicles, most of which are licensed reproductions of real-world automobiles. Since the franchise's debut in 1997, over 90 million units have been sold worldwide, making it the best selling video game franchise under the PlayStation brand.


  15. EA Sports UFC



    a mixed martial arts fighting video game developed in a collaboration between EA Canada and SkyBox Labs, and published by EA Sports for PlayStation 4 and Xbox One. It is based on the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) brand and was released on June 17, 2014. A playable demo was released on the PlayStation Network and Xbox Marketplace on June 3, 2014, with cover athletes Jon "Bones" Jones and Alexander "The Mauler" Gustafsson as playable fighters. It is the first UFC game since THQ sold the license to Electronic Arts.


  16. Mobile Legends: Bang Bang



    a multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) mobile video game developed and published by Moonton, a subsidiary of ByteDance. Released in 2016, the game has grown in popularity around the world, especially in the Southeast Asia region, and has since surpassed 1 billion downloads, with a monthly player peak of 100 million. In 2021, "Mobile Legends: Bang Bang" reached an all-time gross revenue of US$1 billion with 44 percent of its revenue coming from outside Asia, making it the top mobile game of its genre with the most global appeal.


  17. Dota 2



    a 2013 multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) video game by Valve. The game is a sequel to Defense of the Ancients (DotA), a community-created mod for Blizzard Entertainment's Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos. Dota 2 is played in matches between two teams of five players, with each team occupying and defending their own separate base on the map. Each of the ten players independently controls a powerful character known as a "hero" that all have unique abilities and differing styles of play. During a match players collect experience points and items for their heroes to defeat the opposing team's heroes in player versus player combat. A team wins by being the first to destroy the other team's "Ancient", a large structure located within their base.


  18. League of Legends



    a multiplayer online battle arena video game developed and published by Riot Games for Microsoft Windows and macOS. The game is inspired by Warcraft III: The Frozen Throne from the Defense of the Ancients series.


  19. Lokapala



    an online game with the Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA) genre made by Indonesian developer Anantarupa Studios. Lokapala: Saga of the Six Realms is a 5v5 Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA) game focusing on skill and team strategy. The story of Lokapala takes place in the end of the world, when the realms of the thirst, the molded, and the formless are merged into one. The realms are going under the oblivion, waiting for the fate no one can form or escape. The higher dominions of power are now awakened, but only one of them will be able to determine the fate of the realms in this endless fight of powers.


  20. Arena Of Valor



    a multiplayer online battle arena-type video game published by Tencent (through one of its divisions, Timi which is also the sister company of Riot Games, the developer of the PC game League of Legends). As of July 2017, it was reported that the game has more than 80 million daily active players and 100 million active players who have played it. It is the world's best-selling video game and is the most popular free downloadable app.


  21. Five Nights at Freddy's (FNaF)



    an American media franchise created by Scott Cawthon. The video game, Five Nights at Freddy's, started in 2014 and the game became famous worldwide. The game series consists of nine survival horror video games that use locations related to a fictional family pizza restaurant franchise. The restaurant is called Freddy Fazbear's Pizza, named after the animatronic bear, Freddy Fazbear. In most games, the player takes on the role of an employee working at night. The player must use tools such as security cameras, lights, doors, and vents in order to protect the player from the animatronic characters that live in the location. The lore of the series such as Canon is revealed through voice recordings, minigames, and easter eggs displayed through the game. The franchise also encompasses splinter games and other media such as novel trilogies and anthology series, which comprise an entire topic covering the fictional world. This famous franchise maintains an active fanbase due to the production of fan art and games, as well as the sisterhood of these games available worldwide.


  22. Phasmophobia



    a paranormal horror game developed and published by British indie game studio Kinetic Games. It is based primarily on the popular hobby of ghost hunting. The game became available in early access through Steam for Microsoft Windows with virtual reality support in September 2020. The game received a large influx of popularity the following month due to many well-known Twitch streamers and YouTubers playing it, mainly for the Halloween season. On October 15 of the same year, the game was the sixth-most popular game on Twitch. It was the best selling game on Steam globally for several weeks in October to November 2020.


  23. Resident Evil, or Biohazard



    in Japan and Southeast Asia, is a Japanese horror game media franchise created by Capcom. It consists of survival horror, third-person shooter and first-person shooter games, with players typically surviving in environments inhabited by zombies and other frightening creatures. The franchise has expanded into other media, including a live-action film series, animated films, television series, comic books, novels, audio dramas and merchandise. Resident Evil is the highest-grossing horror franchise. The first Resident Evil was created by Shinji Mikami and Tokuro Fujiwara and released for the PlayStation in 1996. It is credited for defining the survival horror genre and returning zombies to popular culture. With Resident Evil 4 (2005), the franchise shifted to more dynamic shooting action, achieved acclaim and influenced the evolution of the survival horror and third-person genres, popularizing the "over-the-shoulder" third-person view.


  24. Pamali: Indonesian Folklore Horror



    a local horror game by StoryTale Studios from Bandung which was released on December 28, 2018. Since the demo version was released, this game has received positive responses and has been eagerly awaited by Indonesian and foreign gamers. The word Pamali itself means taboo, which if violated can bring bad luck or bad impact on someone. Through this game, we can recognize the pamali culture believed by the Indonesian people through the myths in the game 'Pamali' such as the prohibition of cutting down sacred trees and scissors that are said to protect us from kuntilanak.


  25. Dead by Daylight



    an online asymmetric multiplayer survival horror game developed by Canadian studio Behaviour Interactive. It is a one-versus-four game in which one player takes on the role of a Killer and the other four play as Survivors; the Killer must impale each Survivor on sacrificial hooks to appease a malevolent force known as the Entity, while the Survivors have to avoid being caught and power up the exit gates by working together to fix five generators. The game was released for Windows in 2016; PlayStation 4 and Xbox One in 2017; Switch in 2019; and Android, iOS, PlayStation 5, Stadia, and Xbox Series X/S in 2020. It became Steam Deck Verified[b] in 2023. Swedish studio Starbreeze Studios published the game from 2016 through 2018, when Behaviour Interactive bought the publishing rights. Italian company 505 Games publishes the Nintendo Switch version, while Austrian company Deep Silver publishes physical copies for the PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S versions. The game received mixed reviews upon release, though it was a commercial success and has since attracted more than 50 million players. A film adaptation is currently in development by Blumhouse Productions and Atomic Monster.
